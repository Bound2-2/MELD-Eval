1.User Satisfaction: For all key instructions (e.g., answer multiple questions or perform multiple tasks) and explicit constraints (e.g. word count, response length limit, word usage, output format, etc.) provided by the user, the response should be complete in following all of them without any omission.
2.text quality: The response should be grammatically correct, free of spelling errors or typos, use punctuation marks properly and consistently. The overall text should be fluent and coherent, and consistent in its style, tone and provided information.
3.accuracy: For objective questions, the final answer must be correct and accurately address the posed question or problem.
4.Conciseness: The response should be succinct and to the point, avoiding unnecessary elaboration or redundancy. It should contain no more content than necessary to effectively answer the question or fulfill the task, prioritizing clarity and brevity.
5.Completeness: The response should fully address all aspects of the user's question or task, covering all key points and details necessary for a thorough understanding of the topic. It should provide a comprehensive answer that satisfies all elements of the user's inquiry without leaving any part unanswered.
6.Structure: Responses should first accurately present the answer in the requested format, followed by a detailed explanation of the process used to arrive at the answer.
